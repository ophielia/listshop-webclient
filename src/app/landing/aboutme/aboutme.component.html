about the project

At home, I'm a mom. At work, I'm a part of a team of programmers. And in some of the space in between, I've been creating this website and app over the past couple of years.

The idea started with a six month old baby, and an apartment on the second floor without an elevator. When I saw that my grocery store would deliver for free, I jumped on the possibility. The only thing - there was a minimum purchase for free delivery. So I picked out a few dishes, and made a list for a few days, to be sure that I would meet the minimum purchase for delivery. I ended up loving it - not just having the groceries delivered to my door, but knowing what dishes I had shopped for, that I had on hand for making dinner. Being a programmer, I started working on a tool which would save which items I needed for each dish, and could pull them together into a list. That tool was the seed of the list shop.

As I've worked on this project, I've talked to people about how they shop. I found out there are a ton of different ways of filling the cupboards. Some people do a small shopping trip each day. Some people will do a large shopping trip once a month for the staples, and fill in with fresh items once or twice a week. Some people go to the store without a list at all, and just let themselves be inspired by the produce in season or the special deals or even just the moment.

My goal with this project became to make a tool which would be useful for managing shopping list, regardless of your shopping style. If you're a weekly shopper, like I am, you can make a nice weekly list by combining a starter list with the dishes you'll prepare during the week. If you love being inspired as you shop, the list shop can be a tool you can use to lookup at a glance - "What is it that I can make with these fresh tomatoes? What else do I need to make that?"

I hope that you find this tool useful. I welcome any and all feedback.

Happy shopping!
